MODULE 2 — Basic Human Aspirations & Self-Exploration ▼

🎯 Basic Human Aspirations (Universal)

  • ✅ Happiness (Sukh) — to BE always happy
  • ✅ Prosperity (Samridhi) — to have enough
  • ✅ Continuity — of both happiness & prosperity
All other goals (money, fame, job) are just STEPS/PATHS to this basic aspiration — not the goal itself!

⛓️ The 4-Step Effort Chain

Step 4 → BE Happy & Prosperous ← BASIC ASPIRATION
↑
Step 3 → GET / DO something (money, fame, respect)
↑
Step 2 → BECOME something (Engineer, Doctor, IAS)
↑
Step 1 → Present EFFORT (studying, working)
❌ Wrong Way: 1→2→3→4
  • No clarity of goal
  • Direction keeps changing
  • Mix of happiness/unhappiness
✅ Right Way: 4→3→2→1
  • Goal is clear first
  • Plan steps backward
  • Definite direction

🔍 Self-Exploration

  • Inward reflective process to find what is genuinely valuable
  • Dialogue between "What I AM" and "What I REALLY WANT TO BE"
  • Uses Natural Acceptance as the internal compass

🔄 Process of Self-Exploration

Proposal (idea/statement)
↓
Verify via Natural Acceptance
↓
Experiential Validation → Live accordingly
↓
RIGHT UNDERSTANDING → Mutual Happiness & Prosperity

🧠 Natural Acceptance

  • Innate — Part of every human being
  • Invariant — Not corrupted by conditioning, culture, or pressure
  • Universal — Same for all humans
  • Definite — Not subjective or changing

MODULE 3 — Happiness & Prosperity ▼
Happiness = Being in a state of HARMONY
Unhappiness = Being forced into DISHARMONY/Contradiction

❌ Wrong Notions of Happiness

  • Owning/accumulating physical things
  • Pleasure from senses (taste, sound, touch)
  • Seeking attention/appreciation from others
  • Excitement (temporary escape from unhappiness)
⚠️ These lead to: Overeating, Addiction, Violence, Depression, Suicide — NOT happiness!

⚡ Excitement vs Happiness

Excitement (Animal Consciousness)
  • Temporary
  • External stimulus needed
  • Leads to addiction
  • Escape from unhappiness
Happiness (Human Consciousness)
  • Continuous & sustained
  • From inner harmony
  • No dependence
  • Mutual fulfilment

🐄 Physical Facility — Necessary but NOT Sufficient

  • Animal: Physical facility = necessary AND sufficient (cow gets grass → satisfied)
  • Human: Physical facility = necessary BUT NOT sufficient
  • Once humans get food, they immediately think of 100 other things
  • Humans also need: Relationships Right Understanding

CASE STUDIES — Quick Reference ▼

📦 Case 1: Defective Medicine (Pharma Company)

  • Values: Responsibility, Public Welfare, Business Ethics
  • Action: ✅ RECALL medicines — public safety > profit
  • Learning: Social responsibility = protecting public interest over financial gain

🚦 Case 2: Traffic Constable (Law vs Saving Life)

  • Ethical Issue: Act is legally wrong but morally right
  • Mens-rea: Intention = noble (saving life)
  • Action: Allow to hospital → strict warning after → lawlessness must NOT become norm
  • Learning: Moral intention matters, but legality must also be respected — balance both

🏥 Case 3: HIV Positive Employee

  • Values violated: Dignity, Empathy, Rationality, Justice
  • Steps: Don't suspend → Awareness session → Invoke legal protection (HIV Act 2017) → Mediate → Continue work
  • Learning: Conduct driven by fear rather than reason leads to injustice

🎓 Case 4: Ananya's Dilemma (Self-Exploration)

  • Issue: Job offer — aspiration or social expectation?
  • Process: Identify true aspiration → Natural Acceptance test → Verify → Plan 4→3→2→1
  • Key: Aspiration (चाहना) = from within | Expectation (अपेक्षा) = from others

💼 Case 5: Jatin's 80-Hour Work Week

  • Problem: High salary but anxious, poor relationships, no contentment
  • Mistake: Prioritized Physical Facility (3) over Relationships (2) and Right Understanding (1)
  • = Living with Animal Consciousness
  • Solution: Reorder → Right Understanding → Relationships → Physical Facility
